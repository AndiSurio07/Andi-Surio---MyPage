About Me

I began my professional journey in 2015, starting in the BPO industry. Currently, I am working as a freelance web merchandiser for a retail company in Australia. In 9 years of working, I wear different hats, from customer service, Admin logistics, sales support, IT support, recruitment specialist and never thought I will end up doing product administrator job which I didn't expect that I will be good at.

With the current freelance job related to product merchandising, I started with the company doing full time as customer service , taking inbound calls, inbound chat and email, more of online product inquiry and online order follow-ups.

My previous tech experiences have mainly with small computer repair businesses and retail shops, focusing more on hands-on troubleshooting and face-to-face interactions with customers. In my free time, I dedicate myself in studying, reading tech blogs, participating in tech forums, and watching YouTube tech channels. This is my way of keeping myself updated on everything about tech.

In 2009, I successfully completed a two-year diploma program in computer technology, where I gained a comprehensive understanding of various IT concepts and acquired practical skills. Shortly after completing my course, I had the opportunity to work as an IT assistant for a government organization in the Philippines. This role allowed me to apply my knowledge and develop a deeper understanding of IT operations.

In the following year, I was hired by a small business to work as a desktop/laptop technician at a computer service center, where I gained valuable hands-on experience in troubleshooting and resolving technical issues.

During my initial job search, I encountered many employers who preferred candidates with a 4-year degree in IT and experience in corporate organizations or large companies.

This influenced my decision to explore other career paths, and I spent several years working in customer service support roles in the corporate world. Although these experiences have provided a solid foundation for my career, my passion for computer technology has always remained strong, and I am now determined to return to the technical field.
